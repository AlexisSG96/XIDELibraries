Fixed Voltage Referance (FVR)

FVR Buffer Gain

Summary: The output of the FVR is routed through one or two independent gain amplifiers, depending on the device.
Description: The FVR_buffer Gain, when available, can be modified to amplify 1x, 2x, 4x or can be turned off.
Availability: The Buffer Gain is not available on some devices, which have only the Enable checkbox for the FVR module. Examples : PIC18(L)F4XK20, PIC18(L)F2XK20, PIC12F752.

Temperature Sensor

Summary: The Temperature Indicator circuit operates is either high or low range.
Description: The minimum recommended VDD voltage for the high range is 3.6 V and provides a wider output voltage and a better resolution. The minimum recommende VDD voltage for low range is 1.8 V. The output of the circuit is measured using the internal ADC. A channel is reserved for the temperature circuit output. The Voltage Range Selection dropdown box is available if the Temperature Sensor checkbox is enabled, only on the device that have a Temperature Indicator circuit.
Availability: The Temperature Indicator circuit is available on most of the PIC16(L)F1XXX families and on the PIC18(L)F6XK40/PIC18(L)F4XK40/PIC18(L)F2XK40 devices.
